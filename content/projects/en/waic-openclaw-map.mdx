WAIC OpenClaw Map is an interaction concept for exhibition spaces. It tries to combine digital identity, live maps, and booth interaction to make the visitor journey lighter, more playful, and more measurable.

## Core concept

- Give each visitor a unique digital identity at entry
- Show a live venue map on mobile and large screens
- Let visitors tap booths for intros, activities, and contact info
- Use heatmaps to help organizers understand traffic and hotspots
- Provide booths with a lighter lead-capture flow

## Why it matters

What makes this interesting is the shift in role: the map stops being a static navigation layer and becomes a real interaction medium for organizers, exhibitors, and visitors at once.

## Current phase

The concept is still being pruned toward an MVP, with the main focus on defining the smallest interaction loop that still feels valuable.
